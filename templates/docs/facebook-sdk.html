Using Facebook's SDK with Siphon

Siphon now supports Facebook's react-native-fbsdk module. With minimal configuration, you can now integrate your Siphon app with Facebook.

We'll assume you have already created a Siphon app and are ready to use Facebook's SDK. Please follow the quickstart tutorial first if you haven't yet set up your machine to use Siphon.

Note: This module is not supported in the sandbox and is currently available for iOS only. Android support is coming soon.

The react-native-fbsdk module is available for base_version 0.5 or higher. Click here to learn more about base versions.
Register your app with Facebook

Before you can use the react-native-fbsdk module in your project you need to register your app with Facebook.

Log in to your Facebook Developer account and create a new app. Click 'basic setup' to proceed.

Fill in the 'Contact Email' field and select an appropriate category for your app. When you're done, click 'Create App ID'.

Add platform

You need to specify any platforms you wish to support in the 'Settings' section of your app's dashboard on the Facebook Developer site.

iOS

Navigate to the 'Settings' section of your app's dashboard, click the 'Add Platform' button and select 'iOS'.

You need to provide the Bundle ID of your app, which is of the form com.getsiphon.{siphon-app-id} where {siphon-app-id} is replaced with your app's app_id. Your app's app_id is generated by Siphon and is distinct from the app ID generated by Facebook on their Developer site.

To find your app's app_id, navigate to your app's directory and open the .siphon file with a text editor. The {siphon-app-id} corresponds to the value of the app_id field.

Fill in the 'Bundle ID' field of the 'iOS' form. The other fields may be left blank for now.

Important: The 'iPhone Store ID' is generated by Apple when you submit your app to the App Store. Log in to your iTunes Connect account after submitting your app to retrieve this ID, then return to the Facebook Developer site to complete this form.

Android

Android support is coming soon.

Configure your project

You now need to add the app ID created by Facebook to your app's Siphonfile. You can find the app ID on your app's dashboard on the Facebook Developer site.
